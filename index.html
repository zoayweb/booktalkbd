Making Monsters

When I first read Professor David Livingstone Smith's work, I woke up to the terrifying truth that all of us are capable of being cruel. When we know that each of us has the capacity to be cruel (stepping out of our egotistical self-perception), we can choose (and learn) to be kind. Carl Jung warned us: "In each of us there is another whom we do not know." Roger Waters (Pink Floyd) sang, "There's someone in my head, but it's not me." Professor Smith writes extensively about the psychology of dehumanization that worked behind genocides in different parts of the world. Dr. Smith's books made me deeply depressed in the beginning thinking about our human conditions. Later, his work became a tremendous source of my inspiration to be more introspective. I engaged in a dialogue with one of my favorite philosophers: Professor David Livingstone Smith on Aug 22, 2021.

Mother Teresa The Untold Story

Dr. Aroup Chatterjee—a prominent Bengali physician— has been one of the most vocal critics of Mother Teresa. Dr. Chatterjee joined us in a critical conversation about Mother Teresa, which stands in sharp contrast to the West's portrayal of Mother Teresa as a saint. If you read Dr. Chatterjee's book on this issue, it will surely help you to decolonize your imagination in many ways. Dr. Chatterjee was not alone in demystifying Mother Teresa. Christopher Hitchens— notable late British-American author, journalist, orator, and columnist— examined the works of Mother Teresa in a highly skeptical documentary named "Hell's Angel."
