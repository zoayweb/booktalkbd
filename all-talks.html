It gives me immense pleasure to let you know that I will have my first book talk of 2022 with professor David Wengrow, co-author of the book THE DAWN OF EVERYTHING, on January 20. Anthropologist David Graeber, famous for summing up several millennia of economic history in his best-selling "Debt: The First 5,000 Years," spent the last ten years of his life collaborating with the archaeÂ­ologist David Wengrow (Oxford University) on another ambitious project. The two scholars sifted through evidence from 200,000 years of human history to explain how INEQUALITY began. I can't always afford to buy new books out of my pocket, which is why I rely on my university library access in most cases. But I happily paid for this masterpiece. If you purchased Harari's Sapiens and Shoshana Zuboff's The Age of Surveillance Capitalism, buying The Dawn of Everything is worth your every penny.

This book was Professor Graeber's last work. The famed anarchist philosopher, a major figure in the OCCUPY movement, as well as an influential anthropologist who once taught at Yale, died in late 2020. Professor Graber was a fearless intellectual who always spoke truth to the power. Yale notified Graber in the spring of 2005 that his contract would not be renewed next year without giving any reason. But to Dr. Graeber, the reason was obvious: his politics. Speaking truth to the power is costly anywhere in the world! This book is erudite, compelling, generative, and frequently remarkably funny!
