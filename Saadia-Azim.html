About Saadia Azim

“Writing is a like a healing mantra", says Saadia Azim, author of "A Narrow Lane & Other Stories."

“A Narrow Lane & Other Stories” is Saadia's first collection of short stories on relevant issues in contemporary India. "One Day in December" narrates little Asifa's story, and her everyday struggles living under the flyover in a city, overcoming grief and loss. "The Refugee Colony" is the story about young Rana born and living in an un-authorised colony; “Two Gods” is the story of love and anguish in times of religious discord in India.

The title story, "A Narrow lane" is set at area warming up to witness communal riots in the city. "The Killing" is the story about othering people in large communities. The Eye-Witness stands up to testify for real actual criminals.

Saadia Azim is a Culture and Policy Specialist working at the U.S. Mission in India. A former mainstream television journalist, her experiences span over two decades of fieldwork in remote India. A native of Bihar, holding professional management degree from the Chicago Booth School of Business, U.S, Saadia writes fiction and non-fiction for several publications such as the Equator Line, First Post, etc.

Saadia lives in Kolkata with her mixed family of Hindus and Muslims and is proud to showcase the diverse social fabric at home and around.
